Origin of Dish

Lamb chops are a rustic dish with an earthy flavor, frequently served alongside cooked carrots, potatoes, and other root vegetables. Often they're accompanied by mushroom gravy or a balsamic reduction. Believe it or not, humans have been eating lamb chops since the beginning of civilization. Once man came to his senses, baby sheeps became a delicacy and have been there for over 10,000 years.

How To Cook

Ingredients

  • 4 lamb chops, about 1-inch thick
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C)
  2. In a small mixing bowl, combine minced garlic, chopped rosemary, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined.
  3. Place the lamb chops on a baking sheet or in a baking dish 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  4. Brush the garlic and rosemary mixture generously over both sides of the lamb chops.1/4 cup olive oil
  5. 5 Bake the lamb chops for about 25-30 minutes or until the internal temperature reaches 145°F (63°C) for medium-rare or 160°F (71°C) for medium.
  6. Remove the lamb chops from the oven and let them rest for 5 minutes before serving.

Suggested Wine Pairings

Pinot noir, Bordeaux blends, and the Italian reds all pair well with lamb chops, but you can also venture into medium- and full-bodied Cabernet Sauvignon and Merlot if that suits your tastes. These pairings are less common than red blends, however, with red wine blends from the Rhône valley in France being the wines most frequently paired with lamb chops.
